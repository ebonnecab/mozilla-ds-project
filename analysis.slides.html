Correlation Analysis¶

The main question we seek to answer is which individual feature has the most impact on the sales price? To me, the best way to measure that is to examine the relationship between the features and the sales price.

Pandas .corr() function is a very convenient way to find pairwise correlation of the columns within the dataframe by creating a correlation matrix. I'm looking for multicollinearity and features that don't have much correlation with the sale price itself. The only caveat is that this function excludes nonnumerical data.

Conclusion of Part 1¶

At this point, I decided to make my own judgment about which features best describe the sales price. A few features stood out to me, based upon the results from above as well as common sense. Overall quality, the above ground living area in square feet, the year the house was built, and the neighborhood.

Overall Quality¶

The boxplot below shows a clear positive relationship between the overall quality and the sale price. The quality can be ranked from 1 to 10, with 1 being the worst and 10 being excellent. The better the overall quality of the house, the higher the sales price is.

In [126]:
overall_qual = X_train_cat_le["OverallQual"]
price = y_train

Below, we also see a positive relationship between the above ground living area and the sale price. However, I see a few points that have a lot of square feet but the price is still low. This is probably due to issues with other features of the house, which doesn't make the amount of space the house has above ground the strongest predictor in certain cases.

Each of the features that stood out to me were more or less influential but the main feature that seemed to strongly describe the sales price is Overall Quality. I think there are some exceptions in the dataset, but for the most part I see strong growth in the sales price as the quality of the house improves. It may also take in multiple factors that make it a well rounded way to predict how expensive the house will be.

While it may be up for debate, I think the results from the different models/techniques, the graphs, and some domain knowledge supports this claim!
